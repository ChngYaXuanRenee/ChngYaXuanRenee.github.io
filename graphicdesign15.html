Tangled Dreams Zine

Through this process, I’ve explored how weaving, fragmentation, and distortion can convey the emotional and physical toll of insomnia. Using weaving to distort type allowed me to represent the repetitive and trapping nature of sleeplessness, mirroring the endless loops of exhaustion and frustration. This technique added depth to my work, making the distortion of type not just visual but symbolic of my personal struggle. By incorporating fragmented images and overlapping elements, I reflected the complexity of the subconscious mind, breaking down screaming faces into unsettling pieces that evoke disorientation and unease. Combining blurring effects with dreamlike distortions further amplified the hazy, fragmented memories tied to my hallucinations, adding layers of mystery and intrigue. The surrealist elements, such as hearts with mouths, unblinking eyes, and skulls, allowed me to delve into the chaos of sleeplessness. Each symbol, from a speaking heart to the unrelenting hyper-vigilance reflected in skulls, illustrated the inner turmoil of insomnia. The hands clutching my childhood teddy bear became a poignant representation of lost security, showing how my safe spaces were invaded by fear and vulnerability. Incorporating chaotic ink splatters and scribbled lines enhanced the raw, emotional narrative of my work, while collaged skulls captured the uncontrolled chaos of the mind during sleepless nights. These elements, combined with surreal imagery like distorted faces and checkerboard patterns, created a visceral sense of fragmented consciousness and nightmares.

This exploration deepened my understanding of how visual storytelling can convey deeply personal themes. The interplay of weaving, distortion, and surrealism allowed me to communicate the emotional weight of sleeplessness and the complex struggles within my mind. It’s been a journey of turning internal chaos into a tangible narrative, using symbolism and composition to reflect my experiences.
